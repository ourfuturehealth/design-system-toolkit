Maternity and paternity benefits

NHS Low Income Scheme

If you have a low income, you may be able to get help with NHS costs through the NHS Low Income Scheme (LIS).

Universal Credit

On November 1 2015, the arrangements about who's entitled to help with health costs changed. If you receive Universal Credit, make sure you're aware of the new arrangements as you may no longer be entitled to support.

Help with dental costs

Dental charges depend on the treatment you need to keep your mouth, teeth and gums healthy. Find out what you're entitled to and how to get help with NHS dental charges.

Eye care entitlements

Find out if you're entitled to free NHS sight tests or optical vouchers. The section also provides additional information about the current NHS voucher values.

Help with prescription costs

Find out if you're entitled to free prescriptions. Plus learn how to get help with charges, such as through a prescription prepayment certificate (PPC).

Help with travel costs

Find out if and when you can claim refunds under the Healthcare Travel Costs Scheme for travelling to hospitals or other NHS premises.

Help for wigs and fabric supports

Wigs are available on the NHS, but patients are charged for them, unless they qualify for help with charges. Find out if you're eligible for support.
