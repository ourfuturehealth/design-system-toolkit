User needs

AS a user I NEED to know when this information SO THAT I can be reassure that its not out of date information

What's currently being used

To meet NHS England's information standard (https://www.england.nhs.uk/tis/about/the-info-standard/) each product is required to feature production/last review date and the next review due date.

Our hypothesis

We believe

  • Review dates are low on users priority when info seeking, but they would be concerned if the pages hadn't been updated in a number of years.
  • Dates need to sit in context, close to the content they relate to. When they become detached, its unclear what they relate to.

Research findings

Keep it usable, Salford, 4 July 2018 - 5 participants

  • No users reference the review dates, no one commented on content below the pagination.

Page last reviewed: 12/02/2016
Next review due: 01/02/2019

Our hypothesis:

  • The dates need to be alongside the content that they relate to.
  • By moving the dates above the pagination links - we're not detracting from the content or the page to page links.
