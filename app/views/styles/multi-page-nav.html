User needs

As a user I NEED to be able to quickly navigate to a different page in the section SO THAT I can find the most relevant information for my situation.

As a user I NEED to know which part of the website I am currently in SO THAT I don't get lost and I know what other information exists.

What's currently being used

Currently being used on NHS.UK condition pages

Our hypothesis

We believe

  • Having two competing navigations (page-to-page and in-page) is confusing for users.
  • Users miss the page-to-page nav in the blue box as they are drawn to the in-page nav instead.

Iterations

Version 1

Hypothesis

We believe having the page-to-page nav in the right-hand column, and the in-page nav behind a reveal will allow users to navigate easier to other pages within the section.

Research findings

Lab, Leeds, 20th June 2018, 5 users

  • Most users (4/5) missed the link to the page-to-page nav, even when it was on screen, and they were using in-page nav
  • It was not clear to users (4/5) that the section was made up of multiple pages

Version 2

Hypothesis

We believe:

  • removing the in-page nav will not have an adverse effect on users finding/reading the information
  • having the page-to-page nav beneath the page heading will make it clear that the section is made up of multiple pages
  • adding the sub-heading beneath the page heading will indicate that the page has changed
  • adding paging nav at the bottom of the page will allow users to navigate from page-to-page

Prototype for multi-page nav version 2

Research findings

Lab, Manchester, 4th July 2018, 5 users (all with access needs)

Version 3

Hypothesis

We believe:

  • on desktop - having the page-to-page nav in a single column will make it easier to read
  • swapping the heading and sub-heading around will indicate better to users what page they are on
  • removing the horizontal lines between content and navs will make the page flow better
