Warning

User need

As a user I need to identify and understand the important content on the page (even if I don't read all the content) so that I get the best outcome for my situation and don't endanger my health.

Where it's currently being used

  • Conditions - Chickenpox page on nhs.uk
  • Medicines - Amoxicillin page on beta.nhs.uk

Iterations

Version 1

Version 2

Hypothesis

Making the yellow brighter and increasing the font weight, will make it look more like a warning and people will stop and read it.

Research findings

  • 3/5 five users initially missed the callout, with one indicating their attention was drawn to the heading below.
  • Most users correctly understood its importance vs other call outs and the meaning of the yellow colour as a warning.

Version 3

Hypothesis

  • Previous usability testing has shown us that users scan the headings. So adding a heading to the callout will make users stop and take notice.
  • Changing the background colour to yellow will help it stand out from the surrounding white boxes.

Research findings

All users naturally noticed the yellow callout, and 4/5 of the users understood its meaning and place in the hierarchy

Other design considerations

All these designs were peer reviewed but not tested with users.

Usage

The warning callout can be used to display information that is:

  • time critical
  • may have a significant health impact
  • addresses a common and/or significant misconception or patient mistake
