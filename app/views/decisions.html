Grid

The problem

The current NHS.UK grid is a stripped-back version of the first GOV.UK grid.

It had some limitations. These included:

  • a lack of nesting and offsets, and
  • inconsistent break points

Widths and gutter spacing were not appropriate for the NHS.UK font and sizing.

Our thinking

We wanted a grid system that would give us more flexibility and would support earlier versions of Internet Explorer (IE).

We had a look at the first version of the GOV.UK grid and then had a look at various CSS grids, until we came to Bootstrap.

Bootstrap is a popular, tried and tested front-end framework. Our first iteration of the NHS.UK grid system used the Bootstrap 3 grid.

This was due to its support for earlier versions of IE and the familiarity that a lot of developers and designers will have with it (despite their level of front-end development).

We discovered that GOV.UK is currently in BETA with its new front-end design system. This solves a lot of the problems we were trying to address, as well as:

  • allowing us to match GDS design guidelines as much as we can
  • the widths, spacing and breakpoints being closer to what we need
  • to work together with GDS to improve it, as the new GOV.UK design system is based on two-way collaboration, not ultimatum
  • it's not Bootstrap.

Some of the HTML markup for the grid system and its columns will take a bit of practice, but it follows a sensible naming convention and pattern.

We are currently thinking about changing some of the breakpoints for tablet and mobile, due to:

  • GDS mobile breakpoints being quite small, and
  • the increase of larger phones.

Typography

The problems

We found:

  • there was no clear defined sections of content, due to equal spacing between all elements on the page
  • body copy on mobile devices is rather large (same size as on desktop)
  • longer page headings are escaping the container on mobile devices

Our thinking

We might:

  • use single-direction margin declarations, to create clear defined sections of content. This will make the page easier to scan
  • scale down the font size on mobile devices, to make the page a more comfortable read.

Colours

The problems

The current NHS colour palette does not conform to AAA WCAG guidelines 2.0 (including NHS blue).

Our thinking

Tweaking the colour palette will improve contrast between text and its background.

This will allow text to be read more easily by people with moderately low vision.
